What is Volleyball?

Volleyball is an energetic sport made up of two teams. These two teams play with a soft, light ball known as the volleyball. The main goal is to hit this ball to the other side of your teams net on the ground. This is achieved by a couple ways, but there is one main that is popular everywhere. This way is called Bump, Set, Spike! Don't worry if you don't know what this means, I'll teach you everything that a beginner or maybe an intermediate player needs to know before playing the game.

Why People Love Volleyball

Before you learn how to play, I want to show you why people play. Volleyball can be played multiple ways and styles. There is indoor, outdoor, sand volleyball, foot volleyball, etc. each has their own style, but as a main focus I want to show you a more general sense of the game than one direct style. This however, makes volleyball a popular game amoung all ages. Not only can you play anywhere with a net, but you can just practice with friends if you have a ball.

Volleyball is also a non-contact sport, making it apeal to a larger audience than contact sports due to its less-threatening and less-harmful game. Volleyball is also included in the Olympic games as both indoor and outdoor disiplines for both men and women.

Fun Facts

  • Jumping Jackrabbits: During a single competitive match, an active volleyball player can jump up to 300 times to block, spike, or set.
  • Speed of Serve: The fastest recorded serve hit a staggering 83 mph (134 km/h), launched by Poland's Wilfredo Leon.
  • The Unending Rally: Two U.S. teams once kept a single rally alive for 217 consecutive contacts without hitting the floor, lasting over five minutes
