Basic Overview

Volleyball is a team sport played on a two sided court with a net in the middle. Each team will have max of 6 players on the court with three near the net and three near the back of the court away from the net. The game will start by one player "serving" the ball. That player must serve it over the net without steepping over the end line of the court. In this case, the ball will land on the opposite side of the net where the opposing team will try to hit it back over the net.

Pro Tip:

Communication is one of the most important parts of volleyball. Always call the ball to avoid collisions.

Each team will have three hits to try to hit the ball back over the net and land on the ground on the other side. Depending which side the ball lands on, the other team scores a point. Whoever scores the point, serves the ball. When the team who was starting the game by serving get the ball back from the opposing teams serve, that team rotates clockwise for a new server to serve the ball over the net. This will continue until the end of the game. The game will end once one team scores 25 points. However, the game doesn't end quite yet. The overall match isn't done until one team wins either a best of 3 or 5.

General Rules

Here are some of the general rules that apply to the overall sense of the game:

>
  • If the ball hits the ground outside of the lines (wihtout hitting the line itself) the ball is declared dead and the team who hit it out of bounds loses the ball and the opposing team scores a point and starts the new serve
  • The ball must hit inside the court lines for it to count as a point
  • Players must keep their body on their side of the court

    • Ball Hitting Rules

    Volleyball has some strict rules about hitting the ball. These vary between what type of hit it is. Whether the hit is a Serve, Set, Spike or Pass different rules apply. Here are some general rules for hitting the ball:

    General Ball Hitting Rules

    • Max of 3 hits per side
    • Team must alternate hits between players (The same player can hit the ball twice, just not sequentially)
    • No double hits
    • No carrying or catching the ball
    • Players can hit the ball with anypart of their body (feet, head, chest, etc)
    • If a ball is blocked, that does not count as a hit for that side

    Serving Rules

    • General ball hitting rules apply
    • Serve starts the rally
    • Must go over the net
    • No stepping over the line

    Violations

    Violations are a key component for volleyball. Violations are minor rules that keep fairness between teams. Breaking a violation stops the rally immediately and scores a point to the opposing team. Here are a few examples of violations:

    • Hitting the ball out of bounds
    • Touching the net during play
    • Catching or holding the ball
    • Stepping over center line
    • Hitting the ball more than three times on one side
    • A player hits the ballk twice in a row
    • The ball hits the ground on your side
